Tim Berners-Lee's contributions to computer science!

Tim Berners-Lee completely changed the world one day in 1989 when he finished the creation of the world wide web. He also created the first web client and server in 1990. His specifications of URIs, HTTP and HTML were refined as web technology spread.

Sir Berners-Lee was born on June 8, 1955 in London England.b His parents worked on the FERRANTI MARK 1, the first commercial computer. Following his parents steps, he graduated in 1976 from the University of Oxford. He held several positions in the computer industry including a position as a software engineering consultant at CERN, (European Council for Nuclear Research), where he developed procedures that allow computers to communicate with one another. During his career he accumulated numerous honours, including the national academy of engineering prestigious, Charles Stark Draper Prize. In 2004 he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth.
